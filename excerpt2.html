"Listen, do you believe in ghosts?"
"Why?"
Jake smiled. That's better than a flat-out 'no'."
"I never said-"
"You're the first person I ever met who could hear them."
"Hear who?" I stopped and looked around. "Where are we?" I had long since lost track of where Mr. Burnham's grave was, and also the exit.
"I know where we are." Jake's expression was thoughtful. "Corts, you've been hearing voices. Conversations. Who do you think is having them? We're the only ones here. The only living ones," he corrected with a grin.
"I don't know. Not ghosts."
"Why not?"
"Just because. Ghosts? You're crazier than I am."
"I don't think so." There was a change in Jake's voice, a seriousness that brought goose bumps up on my arms.
"What do you mean?" I demanded.
"I don't think you just hear ghosts, Corts. I think you're like me. I think you're haunted."
